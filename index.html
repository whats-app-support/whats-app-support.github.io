How to download or uninstall WhatsApp ?

Download WhatsApp

  1. In the Google Play Store, locate WhatsApp Messenger and select Install.
  2. To accept our Terms of Service, open WhatsApp.
  3. Sign up and confirm your phone number.
  4. Choose Restore if you want to Restore your conversation history from a backup that was discovered.
  5. Add your name and, if you'd like, a profile photo. Additionally, you may modify this later in WhatsApp by tapping More options > Settings, and tapping on your profile name.

Uninstall WhatsApp

  1. Before removing WhatsApp from your device, we advise backing up your messages using the chat backup feature.
  2. Navigate to the Settings on your smartphone.
  3. To delete WhatsApp and all of its data, select Apps & Notifications > WhatsApp > Uninstall.

Note: Please check your device's app store or visit WhatsApp.com/support if your Android device does not support the Google Play Store.

How we choose what to support ?

We frequently analyze and update the operating systems we WhatsApp support because devices and software change often.

We examine which applications and devices are the oldest and have the fewest users each year. Additionally, it's possible that certain smartphones lack the capability needed to operate WhatsApp or may not have the most recent security upgrades .

What happens if your operating system is no longer supported ?

We will notify you via WhatsApp and remind you to update your operating system a few times before we cease WhatsApp supporting it.

This page will be updated frequently to reflect the most recent operating systems that we support.

About rooted phones and custom ROMs

WhatsApp does not support rooted phones or custom ROMs. These adjustments come in too many different forms for us to keep a functional product. Additionally, rooting and custom ROMs prevent WhatsApp's security mechanism from working as intended. Despite the end-to-end encryption, other apps may be able to read your messages if you're using a rooted phone or a custom ROM.

Please remove root and use a stock ROM to get the most out of WhatsApp. For further information on how to unroot your phone, get in touch with the manufacturer. for more information visit whatsapp support

Ending support for WhatsApp on KaiOS

Our goal is to serve as many individuals as possible with a dependable and confidential service.

In order to do this, we frequently assess how WhatsApp is being used, taking into account our expanding feature set that promotes international private messaging and calling.

WhatsApp is no longer supported on KaiOS-powered handsets. All affected users received advance notice before we discontinued support for these devices.

Since there is no backup support or ability to restore from KaiOS devices, users should copy their vital data outside of the device.
